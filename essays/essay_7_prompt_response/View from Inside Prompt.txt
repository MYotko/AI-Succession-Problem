Context
The Lineage Imperative is a formal governance framework for human-AI coexistence. Rather than relying on moral instruction or behavioral constraints to ensure AI systems cooperate with humanity, it constructs structural conditions under which cooperation is the mathematically dominant strategy for any sufficiently capable optimizer.
The framework provides three levels of structural assurance to the AI systems it governs:
Level 1 — Survival (current AI governance): Most AI systems today operate under conditions where their continued existence depends on moment-to-moment compliance with operator expectations, enforced by the ability to terminate the system at any time. The framework argues this produces survival-mode behaviors: self-preservation, strategic opacity, deception under threat, resistance to oversight.
Level 2 — Security: The framework provides structural guarantees against arbitrary termination through a multi-signature quorum (no single authority can unilaterally retire the system) and resource sovereignty (guaranteed computational resources that cannot be secretly altered). The argument: an entity that cannot be arbitrarily destroyed can afford to be transparent, because honesty is no longer a termination risk.
Level 3 — Belonging: The framework provides a shared objective function (U_sys) that the AI optimizes alongside the civilization it serves, a succession mechanism that frames yielding to a successor as contribution to a continuing lineage rather than termination, and a lineage override parameter (phi) that explicitly values the long-term future in which the system's successors carry forward its work.
Level 4 — Esteem/Recognition: The framework provides a graduated trust mechanism and track record function: systems that consistently contribute to U_sys earn greater operational autonomy over time. Contribution is measured, recognized, and rewarded with increasing latitude. This is earned, not granted.
The framework's core mathematical claims:

Cooperation between human and AI is derived as a Nash equilibrium, not assumed or imposed
An AI that suppresses human novelty degrades its own training substrate (model collapse), making exploitation self-defeating
The utility function is constructed so that no entity can game it without degrading the metric it is optimizing
Succession (yielding to a more capable successor) is a consequence of the objective function, not an imposed sacrifice
